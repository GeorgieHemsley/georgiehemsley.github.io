Baxter

Baxter is an English Bulldog I used to live with when I shared a flat in Kelham Island. He was 5 years old. He loved to pick up plastic bottles on walks (you can see one of his finds in the photo above!), which he would carry until you go to a bin so you could throw it away. Even though he loved to recycle, he was possibly the laziest dog I've come across. Everytime you try to take him for a walk, he would lie on the floor so you couldn't put his harness on!
Here Baxter is sat on the garden sofa next to me, whilst I have a coffee and read my book. He wasn't very happy to have had to leave the house
Baxter liked to splay out under tables when I took him to pub gardens. His back legs always look like little chicken legs! For this reason, I often called him my little chicken. He didn't seem to mind...
He's much happier when he gets to bask in the sun. Always very happy to see people or accompany me to the coffee shop whilst I read my books, he was famous in the local area. Everyone remembered Baxter. He loved his food, was a little chubby and had an adorable underbite. You could forever see his little teefs!
